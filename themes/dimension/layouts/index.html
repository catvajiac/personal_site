Research

Saint Mary's College Research

Developing Automated Analysis Codes for Paper Analytical Devices

This summer and fall, I was involved in research at Saint Mary’s College on the PADs project. Many pharmaceuticals that are purchased in the developing world can be substandard, spurious, falsely-labeled, falsified, or counterfeit (SSFFC). There is no global system in place for monitoring medicine quality, but many studies have found expired, fake, or even harmful medicine in pharmacies around the world. Many countries are unable to keep these medicines off the market because of a lack of government control and resources. Saint Mary’s College began the Paper Analytical Devices (PADs) project more than five years ago as a solution to the problem of substandard medication. PADs are pieces of paper about the size of a business card that use a series of colorimetric chemical tests to determine the quality of a pharmaceutical sample. These tests are performed on vertical strips of paper on the PAD, called lanes. For the past few years, trained researchers would visually determine if indicative colors show up on these lanes. In order to globalize the PADs project and allow anybody to interpret the results of a PAD, digital image analysis needed to be implemented. This summer and fall, I continued the research done by two other Saint Mary’s students on developing a software package that would analyze an image of a PAD and tell the user what medication was on the PAD.

Read more about PADs here.
